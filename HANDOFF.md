## 1. What this is

A neutral, plain-language community reference for the New Mexico Medical Psilocybin Advisory Board's **Training & Education** rulemaking. Audience is the whole community, including people who do not understand the legislative process. Goal is clarity, not persuasion: show what was said, who said it, when, and what the options are. **No advocacy. No fabricated rule text. Verbatim quotes only, with attribution.**

The deliverable is becoming a **static website** (GitHub Pages) built from `site/index.html`.

## 3. The three tabled items (the heart of everything)

1. **Controlled-substance (CS) number for diagnostic clinicians.** Should the entry-point "diagnostic clinician" be required to hold a **New Mexico** CS number (clarified 6/26: NM CS number, NOT a federal DEA number)? Source text: **6/12 recommendation draft, p.1**. Not restated in the 6/25 rules draft.
2. **Practicum hours.** Are **100 hours (facilitators) / 120 hours (practitioners)** right, and must all practitioners do **20 supervisory hours**? Source text: **6/12 recommendation draft, pp.7–8**. The 6/25 rules draft touches practicum only via reciprocity.
3. **Reciprocal permit timeline.** Are the sunset dates adequate — **Dec 31, 2026** (reduced-hours 40-contact-hour exception) and **Dec 31, 2027** (department waiver authority)? Source text: **6/25 rules draft, pp.4–5**. NOT in the 6/12 recommendation.

Full verbatim language for all three is in `source-text/raw-pdf-extraction.txt` and rendered in `site/index.html`.

## 4. The two source documents (and how they relate)

- **6/12 recommendation draft** ("Draft for 6-12-26", 9 pp): permit qualifications by role (Diagnostic Clinician incl. **NM CS number**, Practitioner, Facilitator), curriculum modules, **practicum hours (100/120)**, mentoring (10 hrs), record-keeping, continuing ed. **This is what "these recommendations" in the motion refers to — the thing being voted on.**
- **6/25 rules draft** ("Draft Outline for Training and Education Committee review for 6-25-26", 11 pp, 7.35.X.X numbering): educational-program applications, evaluation, third-party evaluators, **reciprocity (incl. both sunset dates)**, testing-out, provider/patient applications, appeals, healing centers, storage, adverse-event reporting. The department's "rules-style" rendering.
- **They are largely COMPLEMENTARY, not two versions of the same text.** Only overlap: Continuing Education (identical). So for each tabled item, one draft usually carries the language and the other does not — that is why the site shows "not restated in this draft" rather than a fabricated parallel.
- Both PDFs are in `Document Register/`. Verbatim text extracted to `source-text/raw-pdf-extraction.txt`.

**The 6/25 confusion (context):** stakeholders were overwhelmed at 6/25 because the department presented the detailed **rules-style** draft instead of the **recommendation-style** doc people were used to. Many conflated the draft with final rules. Gregory: "I can't make them less confused." The site neutralizes this structurally by showing recommendation vs. rules side by side for each item. (No single on-record "you gave us rules not recommendations" quote exists; the closest is Kate Hawk's "tipped into an overload point" at 6/25.)
