# UX review: NMMPAB Training & Education reference site

Reviewed the afternoon of July 17, 2026, against the nine pages in `docs/` as deployed. Frame: the full Advisory Board met this morning (CS number kept, new exam language, hours deferred 7-0); the Training and Education Committee meets at 1:00 PM today. A visitor arriving right now most likely wants: what just happened, what is being decided this afternoon, and where the three items stand.

Scope discipline: everything below preserves the neutral voice, the three visual registers, the no-em-dash rule, and the unified nav and footer. Effort tags: S under an hour, M an afternoon, L more than that.

Move the `.today` strip above the three status cards, make it open by default, delete the scroll auto-close, and lead with the committee meeting. This is the single highest-leverage change on the site today.

3. **Add the one-sentence patient signpost** (Finding 3, S). One line on eligibility.html and one on the hub telling patients and families what this site is and is not, with the four qualifying conditions named and linked.

## Explicitly skip

- **Site search.** Verdict below: not worth it at 9 pages. Over-engineering.
- **Restructuring the nav.** Four groups, three items each, descriptive sub-lines, Community input as the call to action. It works at this page count. Leave it.
- **Any redesign of hours.html, history.html, changes.html, or input.html.** These four pages are doing their jobs. hours.html in particular is the best page on the site: status first, role cards, jump nav, benchmark table with per-row sources, and cost totals honestly left blank as "to research." Leave them alone.
- **A "live updates" or auto-refresh mechanism for meeting days.** The dated "Updated July 17" line plus a promoted recap strip is enough. Anything more is machinery the maintainer has to feed.

## The search verdict

**Do not add search.** Cost: a client-side index (Pagefind, Lunr) adds a build step or a hand-maintained index to a site that currently deploys by pushing static HTML, plus a UI surface, plus the ongoing risk of a stale index silently returning wrong answers on a site whose value is accuracy. Benefit: nine pages, heavily cross-linked, where every high-demand question already has a named destination in the nav, and browser find handles the rest. The one place browser find genuinely fails is pathways.html, because its content is JS-rendered (Finding 8); fixing that is cheaper than search and removes most of the residual case for it. Re-evaluate only if the site grows past roughly 20 pages or starts archiving multiple rulemakings.

## Journeys

**Prospective provider** (say, an LCSW in Las Cruces). Came to learn: can I do this, and what will it take. Needs within 30 seconds: their license row and the hours. First screen on the hub: eyebrow, H1 "The tabled items," a dense process lede, three status cards. Nothing speaks to "you" until the "What's on this site" map cards below the fold. The forced path is two clicks: "Requirements ▾" then "Who can qualify," landing on Table 2, which answers the question well. Where it fails: the first screen is about the process, not the person, and the payoff pages are one dropdown deep. Verdict: works with friction; the map cards mostly rescue it. No structural change needed beyond Findings 4 and 7.

**Patient or family, including end-of-life.** Came to learn: can I (or my dying parent) get this, and when. Needs within 30 seconds: "this site covers provider training rules; patient access is set by the Act; here are the four qualifying conditions; first patients are targeted for December 2027." First screen: whichever page search lands them on, most dangerously "Who can qualify," whose H1 reads exactly like patient eligibility and whose first screen is provider license tables. The qualifying conditions are stated only inside CS-number section 3, the least likely destination for a lay reader. The site never says what it is not. This is the worst journey on the site and the cheapest to fix (Finding 3).

**Board or committee member, stakeholder.** Came for: where each item stands and the record behind it, possibly minutes before the 1 PM meeting. hours.html was explicitly built as their briefing ("Neutral reference for the Training and Education Committee") and it delivers: status box first, the five open questions with attributed positions, benchmarks, cost variables. The hub's three cards give current status in seconds. Verdict: this journey already works. Leave it.

**Journalist or researcher.** Came for: the July 17 outcomes, the 7-0 vote, quotable verbatim language, dates, sources. First screen on the hub: statuses yes, but the meeting narrative is inside a collapsed strip below the cards, and History is behind "The record ▾." Once found, history.html is exactly what they need: newest first, per-entry sources, "Coming up" on top. Finding 1 fixes the discovery gap; History itself needs nothing.

**First-timer who knows nothing.** Came because someone shared a link. Needs within 30 seconds: what this program is, who runs it, what is happening now. First screen: a lede that opens with "The Training & Education Committee wrote a recommendation" and a 3-2 vote from three weeks ago, assuming the reader already knows there is a medical psilocybin program, an Advisory Board, and a rulemaking. The plain-language process explainer exists but is a collapsed details block halfway down history.html. Finding 4 fixes this with one rewritten paragraph and one link.

## The most important thing, right now

**Partially unmissable.** The three status cards do carry today's statuses, dated, in the first screen: "Kept, and going into the rule, July 17," "Deferred by 7-0 vote, July 17." That part works. What is not unmissable is the live half: "the committee meets at 1 PM today" appears only as the last sentence of card 2's body text and inside a collapsed amber `<details>` placed below the cards, which a scroll handler auto-closes if opened. The site's own information architecture calls that strip "Demoted context" in a source comment, which was the right call on July 10 and is the wrong call on the afternoon of July 17.

Minimal fix, no new design: move the existing `.today` strip above the three cards, directly under the "Where things stand" header; set it `open` (or make it a plain non-collapsible strip); delete the auto-close scroll handler; and reorder its summary line so the afternoon meeting leads. It already uses the amber register, already has the "Next:" line with August 14 and the end-of-August hearing target. This is repositioning, not rebuilding, and it stays entirely inside the neutral voice. Effort S.

## Ranked findings

### 1. The day-of story is collapsed, demoted, and self-closing — S
**Evidence:** docs/index.html:362-374 (the `.today` details, collapsed, placed after the cards); index.html:780-785 (scroll handler that closes it); the only first-screen mention of 1 PM is the tail of card 2 (index.html:345); the hero lede (index.html:322) is June 26 history.
**Impact:** the most time-sensitive fact on the site is optional reading. A visitor at 2 PM can leave without learning a decision is being shaped this afternoon.
**Fix:** as described above: move above the cards, default open, remove auto-close, lead with the 1 PM meeting. After the committee meets, the same strip carries the outcome and can be re-demoted next week.

### 2. Who can qualify contradicts the July 17 outcome — S
**Evidence:** eligibility.html Table 2 intro: "The Certifying Clinician column also turns on the Controlled Substance number, which was sent back to the department after July 9." And the legend: "Open: Still unresolved. Sent to committee or back to the department after July 9." Both predate this morning; the page's own header says "Updated July 17" and links to the CS-number update, so the page disagrees with itself.
**Impact:** a reader gets a different state of the world depending on which paragraph they trust. For a site whose method page says "where this site and a primary source disagree, the primary source is right," internal disagreement is the one unforced error.
**Fix:** two sentence edits (before → after below). While there, sweep the other pages for "sent back" phrasings; index and pathways are already updated.

### 3. No signpost for patients and families — S
**Evidence:** no page addresses patients. The nav label "Who can qualify" reads as patient eligibility (its clarifying sub-line "Which licenses map to which permit" shows only inside the open dropdown). The four qualifying conditions appear only in cs-number.html section 3. The December 2027 first-patients target appears only in history.html's "Coming up" note.
**Impact:** the lay half of "the whole community" audience hits license tables and bounces. End-of-life families are the most time-pressed readers this site will ever have.
**Fix:** one scope sentence at the top of eligibility.html and one line on the hub (wording below). No new page, no register change.

### 4. Cold open for first-timers — S
**Evidence:** index.html:322 lede opens with committee history and a 3-2 vote; "How this process works," the site's only plain explainer, is a collapsed block mid-timeline in history.html.
**Impact:** readers who do not follow rulemaking must reverse-engineer the program from committee names before learning why they should care.
**Fix:** rewrite the lede to lead with what and now (before → after below); add a "How this process works →" link beside it or in the site map. Do not move the explainer; link to it.

### 5. "The Tabled Items" is stale and jargon — S
**Evidence:** `<title>` (index.html:6) and H1 (index.html:320). As of July 17 the three items are kept, deferred, and resolved; none is currently "tabled," and "tabled" is insider vocabulary the statuskey never defines.
**Impact:** the site's headline misdescribes its own content in search results, bookmarks, and shares.
**Fix:** retitle (below).

### 6. "Kept" wears the amber "tabled" pill — S
**Evidence:** index.html:337: `<span class="stpill tabled">Kept, and going into the rule, July 17</span>`; CSS defines distinct sent/tabled/resolved pill styles (index.html:82-84). Amber means "still in play" everywhere else (the ◆ Open legend on pathways, the diffbox register).
**Impact:** at a glance, items 1 and 2 look like the same state; they are now fundamentally different (department decision made vs. actively being reworked at 1 PM).
**Fix:** a neutral slate pill for "kept" (reuse `--pillN` tokens), and shorten the text: "Kept by the department · July 17." Keep the statuskey definitions in sync.

### 7. "Cost" is invisible from the hub — S
**Evidence:** the word "cost" appears on the hub only inside item 2's dense whatnow paragraph (index.html:555). The Training hours map card (index.html:408-413) describes didactic and specialization hours only. The actual cost content at hours.html#cost is excellent and honest (no NM analysis yet; variables and out-of-state reference prices).
**Impact:** one of the five highest-demand questions has no visible entry point.
**Fix:** one card-description edit (below), optionally plus a "What it may cost →" link in card 2's link row.

### 8. pathways.html content exists only in JavaScript — S (fallback) / M (pre-render)
**Evidence:** docs/pathways.html:291 is an empty `<div id="starts">`; every starting license and route step is built by script (pathways.html:364-513); there is no `<noscript>`.
**Impact:** browser find cannot find the steps, search engines cannot index them, and a JS failure leaves a blank page between the hero and the Options-raised zone. This is also most of the residual argument for site search.
**Fix now:** a `<noscript>` paragraph pointing to eligibility.html, which holds the same data in static tables. **Fix later:** pre-render the default selection's markup in the HTML and let the script enhance it.

### 9. about.html no longer describes the site — S
**Evidence:** about.html says "Updated July 10, 2026" and lists as transcript sources only June 25, June 26, and July 9. The site now also relies on the July 16 End-of-Life committee meeting (all three Options-raised zones) and the July 17 board meeting, including verbatim rule language quoted from speech with the note "an updated draft document has not been posted" (cs-number.html). Neither meeting appears in about.html or the Documents menu.
**Impact:** the trust page undercuts the chain-of-source promise precisely where the newest and least-verifiable material entered.
**Fix:** add both meetings to the method section with their verification tier (they belong in the middle "Transcript" tier, or a stated "from the live meeting, recording pending" tier), and add a "Transcript · July 17: pending" placeholder line to the Documents dropdown so the promise stays visibly honest.

### 10. The hub duplicates the item pages' full evidence records — M, consider next
**Evidence:** index items 1-3 embed complete quote records (10, 12, and 4 quotes; index.html:453-537, 564-662, 689-734) that also live on cs-number.html and hours.html. Three copies of every status line is exactly how Finding 2's drift happens; the hub is ~800 lines.
**Impact:** every meeting now requires the same fact to be updated in up to three places, on a site maintained by one time-poor person.
**Fix:** when the cycle slows, cut each hub item to whatnow + diffbox + "Where it stands" + a link, and let the item pages own the records. Not this week.

## Wording: before → after

All rewrites are to the site's own prose. No verbatim quotes or draft language are touched.

**1. Hub lede (index.html:322)** — buried lead, assumes context.
Before: "The Training & Education Committee wrote a recommendation for how medical psilocybin providers are trained and permitted. On June 26, 2026 the Advisory Board voted 3-2 to accept it and set three items aside. The board took those three items up at its July 9 meeting and again at its July 17, 2026 meeting."
After: "New Mexico is writing the rules for who may provide medical psilocybin therapy and how they are trained. Three items are still open. The full Advisory Board met this morning, July 17; the Training and Education Committee meets at 1 PM today to work on the training hours. How the process works is on the History page."

**2. Title and H1 (index.html:6, 320)** — stale jargon.
Before: "Training & Education: The tabled items" / "NM Medical Psilocybin Training Rules: The Tabled Items"
After: "Training & Education rules: the three open items" / "NM Medical Psilocybin Training Rules: The Three Open Items" (still accurate after today: one kept but not final, one in committee, one resolved but not yet written in).

**3. Today-strip summary (index.html:363)** — one 45-word sentence, afternoon buried.
Before: "The July 17 board meeting, in brief: the board voted 7-0 to send the practicum and didactic hours to the Training and Education Committee; the department is keeping the certifying-clinician controlled-substance-number requirement and revised the exam language."
After: "This morning, July 17: the board sent the practicum and didactic hours to the Training and Education Committee by a 7-0 vote. The department is keeping the certifying-clinician controlled-substance-number requirement and revised the exam language. At 1 PM today: that committee meets to work on the hours."

**4. Eligibility Table 2 intro** — stale.
Before: "The Certifying Clinician column also turns on the Controlled Substance number, which was sent back to the department after July 9."
After: "The Certifying Clinician column also turns on the Controlled Substance number, which the department kept in the rule on July 17. Not final; it goes to a public hearing."

**5. Eligibility legend, "Open"** — stale definition.
Before: "Open: Still unresolved. Sent to committee or back to the department after July 9."
After: "Open: still unresolved as of July 17, with the committee or the department."

**6. Patient signpost (new, one sentence, eligibility.html hero; short variant on the hub)**
"These tables cover provider licenses, not patient eligibility. The Act's qualifying conditions for patients are PTSD, major treatment-resistant depression, substance use disorders, and end-of-life care; how a patient is certified is on the CS number page."

**7. Item 2 card body (index.html:345)** — parenthetical arithmetic defeats glancing.
Before: "100 / 120 practicum hours and the didactic modules (25 core + 5 role-specific + 5 simulated, about 35 hours combined) stand unchanged in the draft (p. 4-6)."
After: "About 35 classroom hours, plus 100 supervised-practice hours for facilitators and 120 for practitioners, stand unchanged in the draft (p. 4-6)."

**8. Training hours map card (index.html:411)** — make cost findable.
Before: "Didactic, practicum, and the proposed end-of-life specialization hours."
After: "The proposed hours, what other states require, and what training may cost."
