### EOL as a role overlay (cleaner statement of the structural principle)
EOL is not its own license domain. It is a cross-cutting specialization/endorsement that
overlays the three roles (Clinician, Practitioner, Guide/Facilitator). The same EOL
competency can land in a clinical, behavioral-health, or community context depending on the
role carrying it. Model it as an overlay/tag on roles, not a separate license row. The
chaplain/EOL-doula continuity gap (no post-training slot) is the load-bearing example.

### Baseline vs specialization, by curriculum domain (Jenn's first-pass tagging)
Baseline for all facilitators (the psychospiritual core):
- Spiritual assessment and meaning-making
- Grief, loss, and bereavement
- Family and relational systems
- Ethics, culture, equity, informed consent
EOL specialization overlay:
- Client mindset in serious illness and EOL
- Medical complexity and safety
- Adapting facilitation for EOL care
- Interdisciplinary collaboration and sustainable practice
Rule of thumb: psychospiritual content is baseline for everyone; medical-complexity and
prognosis-aware content is the EOL specialization layer. For each baseline domain the
overlay defines the EOL-specific addition; for each EOL domain, name the baseline
prerequisite assumed before the module.

### Curriculum shape (for the hours page specialization addendum)
Proposed 9-session curriculum, roughly 15 to 20 total hours, organized as a
competency/session/assessment matrix (domain, hours, faculty profile, primary focus,
suggested assessment). Present as a proposal under discussion, not a settled requirement.

### Open policy questions (restated from the main brief)
Baked-into-core vs. separate add-on for EOL hours (unresolved); test-out/credential-out for
already-specialized palliative practitioners; DOH prefers endorsements and best-practice
recommendations over diagnosis-specific licensure, with a professional organization floated
as the long-term home; EOL is not an appropriate starting practicum population, progression
is resident to co-facilitator to supervised primary.
