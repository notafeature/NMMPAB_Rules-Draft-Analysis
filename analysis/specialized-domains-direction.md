## The idea

Specialized-domain care is a layer on top of the core provider training. End-of-life
is the first specialized domain. The other qualifying conditions (PTSD, substance
use disorders, treatment-resistant depression, and conditions the department later
approves) are the same shape: each can be seen as a domain a provider specializes in.

The open structural question: are the practicum and didactic hours toward a
specialization written INTO the core educational requirements, or are they ADDENDA
layered on top of a core curriculum. Gregory's framing on July 10: training hours for
a specialization read best as an addendum to a specialized route, not folded into the
core count.

## Where it shows up (Gregory named these three pages)

1. Provider Routes (docs/pathways.html): specialization as an optional route layered
   on a core permit. A person picks a starting license, reaches a core permit, and can
   then add a specialized-domain route (end-of-life first, other domains shown as
   options).
2. Who can qualify (docs/eligibility.html): reflect specialization as an option
   alongside the core permit eligibility, so a reader sees that specialized-domain
   competence is an added layer, not a separate license.
3. Training hours (docs/hours.html): specialization hours presented as an ADDENDUM to
   a specialized route, distinct from the core practicum and didactic totals. Show all
   specializations as options to reflect on, not just end-of-life.

## Framing (read this twice, it is the part most likely to go wrong)

This content changes register, and the change must be deliberate and visible.

- Until now every page holds one register: the neutral record of dated facts and
  attributed quotes. This new material is a second, clearly separated register:
  possibilities and progressions that emerged in the July 9 conversation, offered for
  consideration.
- It is NOT a directive and NOT the site's recommendation. Present each specialization
  and the core-vs-addendum structure as options a reader or the committee might weigh,
  never as what the rule should do.
- Keep the site's own voice neutral. Gregory's word "valuable" describes his read of
  the conversation, not the page's voice. On the page, enthusiasm and endorsement are
  attributed to named people who said them, with date and quote. The site surfaces; it
  does not praise.
- Demarcate it. A reader must be able to tell at a glance where the neutral record ends
  and "options to consider" begins. Use a distinct, labeled treatment (a clearly marked
  section or card style) so the two registers never blur.
- "Include all specializations as options to reflect on": end-of-life leads because it
  is furthest along in the record, but the structure should show the full set of
  qualifying-condition domains as parallel options, not a single endorsed one.
