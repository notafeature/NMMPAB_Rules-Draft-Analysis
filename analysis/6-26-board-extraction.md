## The three tabled items (6/26)

### a. CS number
- **Dominic Zurlo** (defend, segs 34–36): "while this is a Schedule I at this time, because we are treating it in other ways as a medication, it still needs to have that medical clearance to be utilized in a therapy type practice." — "we have had thousands of providers who have referred patients to the program. We are not asking those providers to prescribe psilocybin... We do not see this as any different."
- **Board member** (CS≠DEA, seg 61, **[speaker uncertain]**): "number is not a DEA. So we're saying we want the controlled substance number. We don't require the DEA... Just making sure that that was intentional." — **Zurlo (62):** "Yes... It is the same requirement that we have with regard to cannabis."
- **Denali Wilson** (Healing Advocacy Fund, segs 63–68): "the statute says specifically that the medical appropriateness of a candidate is based solely on whether they have a diagnosis with a qualifying condition." — "We're setting ourselves up to create a really small pool of providers... I don't believe that this design is supported by the statute because it goes beyond the requirement of diagnosis." — "it looks like we're also asking this diagnostic clinician to do a risk benefit analysis... putting them in a really difficult position billing-wise."
- **Denali Wilson** (property interest, segs 103–104): "if diagnostic clinician is a permit group, we've created a property interest for a group of people who have a financial interest now in providing that care... That is a much more difficult thing to remedy down the line."
- **Chris Peskuski** (segs 76–77, 106–107): "I'm very concerned that this is going to bottleneck access, especially for veterans... I get about four minutes before his little buzzer goes off." — "Colorado and Oregon have both shown this can be done effectively... any problematic medical conditions can be referred from their side instead of requiring a medical screening first." — "None of this adds up to me honestly."
- **Dan Jennings** (segs 79–80): "as being in the very rural area here, I worry about that there won't be enough of the providers that number one would be in this pool and then taking it down to having a CS number and then it just even being in my area."
- **Ian Dunn** (segs 85–87): "This person's job is just to ensure physical safety, that there's no bad drug interactions, that there's no condition that is going to disqualify them or put them at risk."
- **Alana Dances** (segs 90–96): "you need a clinician to say, this person has PTSD, this person does not have cardiac disease... The clinician doesn't have to say... I think psilocybin is the correct treatment." — "I find this to be a really nice balance of protecting safety on a brand new program... when you move too fast, you hurt patients. Like scaling programs is where we hurt people."

### b. Practicum hours
- **Dominic Zurlo** (segs 168–169): "the supervised practice will be **100 hours for facilitators and 120 for practitioners**. Students would participate in a minimum of 80 hours of administration and same-day therapy sessions... with a minimum of 14 different patients over eight different... sessions... broken up into six individual and two group sessions." — "practitioners would need to complete an additional 20 hours... for the purpose of being able to supervise facilitators."
- **Brenda Burgard** (segs 171, 174–175): "this is just gonna be a separate program just for psilocybin supervisors, the 20 hours." — "so many people are going, well, wait a minute, I'm a supervisor already as a clinician. Do I have to do this extra 20 hours... And what you're saying is yes."
- **Larry Leeman** (segs 180–182): "Several people in this state have gone up to Colorado and done a practicum, which took three days... The cheapest one is over $2,000. And so I'm wondering about what are the expenses. I think it would be worth trying to model how we think that's going to occur." — "before anyone be approved to sit with people with end of life and PTSD that they have [experience] in those specific diagnoses."
- **Scott Shannon** (public; CO psychiatrist, segs 197–198): "40 hours of practicum is probably excessive from what we're seeing... I would encourage a less cautious and conservative approach to this model to try to really emphasize access."
- **Dominic Zurlo** (seg 199): "the timeframes on this actually have come from various recommendations... including people who have been doing this work underground... this is not just arbitrary."
- **Shane** (dept/participant, segs 203–204): "when a program is started With those kind of modalities that we're going to change in the future. The change in the future, Never happens... I have low confidence that that's going to happen correctly."
