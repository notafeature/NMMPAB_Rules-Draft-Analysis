Two words to hold precisely

Adopted = only the single motion above (the two drafts as a package, minus the three carve-outs). The board did not vote on the individual edits below.

Verbal agreement = a change people agreed to in discussion, with no final dictated wording yet. These are labeled “verbal agreement, no final text.” They are not finished rule language and are not final until the department prints the rules.

The two draft documents

Which text governs

Two working drafts were in play. Neither is final promulgated rule text. Where they cover the same topic and differ, the later 6/25 version is treated as the current wording.

Draft 1 — 6/12/26 recommendation draft

Recommendation-style, 9 pages. Covers permit requirements by role (Diagnostic Clinician, Practitioner, Facilitator, including the New Mexico Controlled Substance number), curriculum requirements, practicum hours (100 / 120), mentoring, record-keeping, and continuing education. The earlier source.

Draft 2 — 6/25/26 rules-style draft controlling text

Rules-style, 11 pages, using 7.35.X.X numbering. Covers educational-program application and evaluation, third-party evaluation, reciprocity (including the Dec 31 2026 and Dec 31 2027 exceptions), testing-out, provider and patient applications, appeals and discipline, healing centers, other approved locations, storage, and adverse-event / complaint reporting. The later, controlling text, presented at the 6/25 rules hearing.

Note for context: at the 6/25 hearing, several attendees said the newly detailed document arrived close to the meeting and raised many questions. The department framed it as a pre-hearing draft, not a finished rule: “This is not the only time that these are going to be reviewed… there is time for changes. This is the start.” (Dominic Zerlo, 6/25)

What was adopted

The framework the board approved on 6/26

The adopted framework is the 6/12 recommendation draft plus the 6/25 rules draft, as amended in discussion, with the three carve-outs removed and sent to July 9. Everything in those two drafts except the three carve-outs is inside the adopted package. The line edits discussed are verbal agreements to be checked against the printed rules, not separately adopted text.

The three carve-outs

Removed from the motion, not deleted — sent to the July 9 session

1Controlled substance (CS) number for diagnostic cliniciansThe requirement that a diagnostic clinician hold a New Mexico controlled substance number. Clarified on 6/26 as a New Mexico CS number, not a DEA number.
2Practicum hour requirementsThe 100-hour (facilitator) / 120-hour (practitioner) practicum and its sub-requirements, including the 20-hour supervisory practicum.
3Reciprocal permit timelineThe sunset dates in the reciprocity section (Dec 31 2026 for the 40-contact-hour exception; Dec 31 2027 for the department’s waiver authority).

Full verbatim draft language and the attributed discussion for all three are in Document 2 — The Three Open Items for July 9.

Verbal agreements (redline)

Agreed in discussion on 6/25 or 6/26 — none are adopted rule text

These are changes people agreed to in discussion. None were separately voted on; each is a verbal agreement to be mapped against the actual rules when the department prints them. Read them as “agreed, no final text yet.”

Provider roles & training

  • Diagnostic clinician continuing education: annually → every two years Drafting correction noted by Dominic on the record · 6/26
  • Wilderness first aid: required simply for sessions in a natural environment → required based on EMS access / remoteness (roughly more than an hour from EMS) Chris Piskuski proposed; Larry Lima agreed; Dominic “added that on in” · 6/26 · verbal agreement, no final text
  • Facilitator curriculum: add peer-support training as an option alongside the therapeutic-101 overview Chris Piskuski; Dominic agreed · 6/26 · verbal agreement, no final text
  • Facilitator ethics / therapeutic-101: add personal bias and the patient Bill of Rights James Brown / Ian Dunn; Dominic · 6/26 · verbal agreement, no final text

Educational program approval & evaluation

  • Faculty experience: the two required faculty must have experience with 40 individual patients 6/26 add-on; how it is determined/demonstrated flagged as open. Overlaps the 6/25 “40 unique patient sessions” practicum-instructor placeholder · verbal agreement, no final text
  • Third-party evaluation: add CME/CEU approval as an alternative pathway (both pathways remain available) Raised by Ann Metz, Catherine Warnock, Dr. Fatimi, Lisa Ginsberg; department agreed · 6/25, explored again 6/26 · verbal agreement, no final text
  • Inspections: exempt active practicum / dosing sessions from unannounced inspection — notice required for those Catherine Warnock, Dr. Fatimi; Dominic · 6/25 · verbal agreement, no final text

Reciprocity (non-timeline parts)

  • Non-US programs: add requirement that non-English documentation be translated and apostilled Ian Dunn; Dominic agreed · 6/26 · verbal agreement, no final text
  • Clarify that the 40 contact hours are still required even for otherwise-exempt early applicants Clarification stated by the department in response to Ann Metz · 6/25 · verbal agreement, no final text

Healing centers & other approved locations

  • Mobile treatment units: restore the accidentally deleted mobile-unit language under “other approved locations” Chris Piskuski flagged; Dominic: “I will find that and we will add that back on in” · 6/25
  • Medical necessity for home / alternate location: clarify the care team (diagnostic clinician with practitioner) determines it Chat question (Jessica); Dominic · 6/25 · verbal agreement, no final text
  • Non-permitted staff present during treatment: clarify they must be covered in the patient consent form Raised 6/25; Dominic to clarify in the rules · 6/26 · verbal agreement, no final text
  • Add the patient Bill of Rights to healing-center responsibilities James Brown via Dominic · 6/26
  • Language access: add translation / language-access requirements (English + Spanish; ADA / Language Access Act) Skye via Dominic · 6/26 · verbal agreement, no final text

Reporting

  • Adverse health event report: add product ID as a required field Raised by Greg (and Hanifa via chat) at 6/25; reflected in the healing-center reporting language presented 6/26 · verbal agreement, no final text
