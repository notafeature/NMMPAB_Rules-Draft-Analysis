New Mexico Medical Psilocybin Program · Advisory Board

Training & Education rules: the three tabled items for July 9, 2026

New Mexico is adopting rules for how medical psilocybin providers are trained and permitted. On June 26, 2026 the Advisory Board voted 3-2 to adopt the Training & Education framework but excluded three items. Those three items are scheduled for July 9, 2026, which DOH now lists as a full Advisory Board meeting, so the board can vote on them.

1

The controlled-substance numberCommittee recommendation · Diagnostic Clinician qualifications

Should a diagnostic clinician be required to hold a New Mexico controlled substance number to be permitted?

Context: the diagnostic clinician authorizes a patient's entry to the program. A controlled-substance-number requirement limits which clinicians can serve in that role. Clarified on 6/26 as a New Mexico CS number, not a federal DEA number.

Positions on the record

Stated in support of the requirement

  • Dominic Zurlo (department), 6/26: “it still needs to have that medical clearance to be utilized in a therapy type practice… We do not see this as any different” from the cannabis model.
  • Ian Dunn (board chair), 6/26: “This person's job is just to ensure physical safety, that there's no bad drug interactions, that there's no condition that is going to disqualify them.”
  • Alana Dances (Acting Medicaid Director), 6/26: “a really nice balance of protecting safety on a brand new program… when you move too fast, you hurt patients.”
  • Dr. Truckner (department physician), 6/25: “the DEA license has nothing to do with my responsibilities to clear this patient safely.”

Stated in support of changing it

  • Denali Wilson (Healing Advocacy Fund), 6/26: “the medical appropriateness of a candidate is based solely on whether they have a diagnosis with a qualifying condition… it goes beyond the requirement of diagnosis.”
  • Chris Peskuski (board member), 6/26: “this is going to bottleneck access, especially for veterans… Colorado and Oregon have both shown this can be done effectively.”
  • Dan Jennings (board member, rural), 6/26: “I worry… there won't be enough of the providers… taking it down to having a CS number and then it just even being in my area.”
  • Dr. Fatemi (training-program operator), 6/25: “I don't know any… other physicians… who would be also taking the risk… of having a DEA license.”

The language on the table

Committee recommendation · 6/12what's being voted on

Diagnostic Clinician: qualifications required to certify patients:

  • “Have a current and valid license from a recognized New Mexico licensing board which allows diagnosing the qualifying medical condition within their licensure scope of practice…; and,”
  • “Have a New Mexico Controlled Substance number; and,”
  • “Complete required NMDOH training and continuing educational training requirements annually; and,”
  • “Apply, receive, and maintain a valid permit from the NMDOH medical psilocybin program as a diagnostic clinician.”

Verbatim from the 6/12 recommendation draft, p. 1.

Rules draft · 6/25for reference

The 6/25 rules draft does not restate the diagnostic-clinician qualifications. It covers the provider application process; the CS-number requirement lives only in the 6/12 recommendation above.

Confirmed against the 6/25 rules draft, provider application section, pp. 6–7.

Proposed ways to proceed

Each option is tied to a statement on the record. Where redline wording is shown, no exact rule text was dictated at the meeting; it renders the attributed proposal. These are options that were raised, not a recommendation.

1Keep the requirement as written

Department position — Dominic Zurlo, 6/26: “We do not see this as any different” from the cannabis model.

The 6/12 diagnostic-clinician qualification stands unchanged.
2Remove the requirement; base entry on diagnosis alone

Raised by Denali Wilson, 6/26: eligibility “is based solely on whether they have a diagnosis with a qualifying condition.” No replacement wording was dictated on the record.

• Have a New Mexico Controlled Substance number; and,
• Complete required NMDOH training…

Rendering of the attributed proposal, not official text.

3Allow medical clearance by referral instead of requiring the CS number at entry

Raised by Chris Peskuski, 6/26: “any problematic medical conditions can be referred from their side instead of requiring a medical screening first.”

No rule text was proposed on the record; shown as stated.

The practicum hoursCommittee recommendation · Practicum Requirements

Are 100 hours (facilitators) / 120 hours (practitioners) right, and should every practitioner complete 20 supervisory hours?

Context: the practicum is the supervised-practice portion of provider training. The number of required hours affects the cost and time to qualify.

Keep the hours as written

  • The timeframes came from practitioners, including underground providers; “not just arbitrary.”
  • This is a medical program treating people with qualifying conditions, unlike CO/OR general access.
  • Rules can be revised later if needed.

Department (Dominic Zurlo)

Reduce, phase, or make supervision optional

  • CO/OR data show reduced volume from cost and duration; “40 hours of practicum is probably excessive.”
  • A Colorado practicum runs over $2,000; the economics need modeling.
  • The 20 supervisory hours are effectively uninsurable and should be opt-in.

Scott Shannon, Larry Leeman, Dr. Ann Metz, Catherine Warnock

The language on the table

Committee recommendation · 6/12what's being voted on
  • “Supervised practice training… must include a minimum of one hundred (100) hours… for Facilitators and one hundred and twenty (120) hours for Practitioners.”
  • “Students shall participate in a minimum of eighty (80) hours of administration and same-day therapy sessions… with a minimum of fourteen (14) different patients over a minimum of 8 different… sessions” (≥6 individual with ≥6 patients; ≥2 group with 4+ each).
  • “Students shall participate in a minimum of twenty (20) hours of in-person preparation and post-integration therapy sessions” (≥6 patients; ≥1 group prep and ≥1 group post-integration).
  • “Practitioners must complete an additional minimum of twenty (20) hours as a practitioner supervising facilitators…”
  • “All practicum hours must take place in an approved Healing Center.”

Verbatim from the 6/12 recommendation draft, pp. 7-8.

Rules draft · 6/25for reference

The 6/25 rules draft does not restate the practicum hours. It touches practicum only through reciprocity: a “40 hours of contact time” exception, and a requirement that reciprocal practitioners still “complete the twenty (20) supervisory practicum hours in New Mexico.”

Confirmed against the 6/25 rules draft, reciprocity section, pp. 4–5. The 100 / 120 figures were read aloud by the department at the 6/26 board meeting.

Ways it could be written

AKeep 100 / 120 as written

Reflects the department.

The full practicum section stands, revisited later if data warrant.
BLower or phase the hours

Reflects Scott Shannon (“probably excessive”) and Larry Leeman (cost modeling).

“…a minimum of one hundred (100)[reduced number] hours… for Facilitators and one hundred and twenty (120)[reduced number] hours for Practitioners, with an initial phase-in period reviewed against cost and volume data.”

Illustrative drafting option, not official text. Leaves the exact reduced number for the board to set after economic modeling.

CMake the 20 supervisory hours opt-in

Reflects Dr. Ann Metz's “practitioner supervisor credential” and the liability-insurance gap.

“Practitioners must completewho wish to supervise facilitators may complete an additional minimum of twenty (20) hours… supervising facilitators, as an optional supervisor credential.”

Illustrative drafting option, not official text.

DAdd condition-specific & instructor-experience floors

Reflects Larry Leeman (end-of-life / PTSD experience) and Catherine Warnock (instructors should exceed the minimums they teach; a ~40-patient floor).

Before sitting with end-of-life or PTSD patients, a provider must document experience in those specific diagnoses. Practicum instructors must have experience with a minimum of 40 individual patients.

Illustrative drafting option, not official text. Overlaps the 6/26 faculty-experience add-on.

The reciprocity timelineRules draft · Reciprocity, pp. 4–5

Are the sunset dates adequate: Dec 31, 2026 for the reduced-hours exception, and Dec 31, 2027 for the department's waiver authority?

Context: reciprocity lets providers trained in other government-approved programs (for example, Colorado or Oregon) qualify in New Mexico under reduced practicum requirements. The sunset dates set how long those reduced requirements remain available.

Keep the dates as written

  • A sunset clause prevents permanently reduced requirements.
  • Waiver authority lets the department build infrastructure without new rulemaking.

Department (Dominic Zurlo)

Extend or rethink the dates

  • Healing centers don't exist yet; Dec 2026 may be unrealistic.
  • The department itself noted the timeline “could be extended.”
  • Concern it “encourages people to get trained in Colorado.”

Larry Leeman; department open to extension

The language on the table

Committee recommendation · 6/12for reference

Not in the 6/12 recommendation. Reciprocity and its timeline exist only in the 6/25 rules draft.

Confirmed against the 6/12 recommendation draft (no reciprocity section).

Rules draft · 6/25the text at issue
  • “Exception: any reciprocal applications received by the department by December 31, 2026 will not be required to complete the full practicum hours required by New Mexico, but must demonstrate completion of at least 40 hours of contact time with a minimum of 2 separate individual sessions or 2 separate group sessions…”
  • “Exception: The department reserves the ability to waive, temporarily suspend, or reduce any of the rules regarding the practicum requirements… up until December 31, 2027; in order to facilitate the permitting of individuals trained by other governmental approved programs to build the initial infrastructure of the program.”

Verbatim from the 6/25 rules draft, reciprocity, pp. 4-5.

Ways it could be written

AKeep Dec 31, 2026 / Dec 31, 2027

Reflects the department.

Both sunset dates stand as drafted.
BExtend the sunset dates

Reflects Larry Leeman's timing concern and the department noting it “could be extended.”

“…received by the department by December 31, 2026[later date]…”  ·  “…up until December 31, 2027[later date]…”

Illustrative drafting option, not official text. Keeps the sunset structure but moves the dates out.

CClarify the 40-hour requirement (already agreed)

A clarification the department stated at 6/25 in response to Dr. Ann Metz.

Applicants otherwise exempt from the full practicum must still demonstrate the 40 contact hours.

Verbal agreement, no final text yet. Separate from the sunset-date question.
