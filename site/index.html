New Mexico Medical Psilocybin Program · Advisory Board

Training & Education:
the three tabled items for July 9, 2026

The Training & Education Committee developed a recommendation for how medical psilocybin providers are trained and permitted. On June 26, 2026 the Advisory Board voted 3-2 to accept the recommendation, but set three items aside. Those three items go to a session on July 9, 2026.

July 9 was scheduled as a committee meeting, not a board vote. On the record at the June 26 Advisory Board meeting, the July 9 session (9-11 AM) was defined as an Education & Training Committee meeting to discuss the three tabled items, ahead of the full Advisory Board meeting on July 17. A committee discusses and refines; the Advisory Board is the body that votes. (DOH's public meetings page currently lists July 9 as a full Advisory Board meeting.)

1

The controlled-substance numberDiagnostic Clinician qualifications

Should a diagnostic clinician be required to hold a New Mexico controlled substance number to be permitted?

What this is: the diagnostic clinician authorizes a patient's entry to the program. A controlled-substance-number requirement limits which clinicians can serve in that role. Clarified on June 26 as a New Mexico CS number, not a federal DEA number.

Where the two documents differ

The June 12 recommendation (p. 1) lists a New Mexico Controlled Substance number as a required qualification. The June 25 rules draft (pp. 6-7) does not restate the diagnostic-clinician qualifications, so this requirement appears in the recommendation only.

1The recommendationJune 12, as written

Diagnostic Clinician: qualifications required to certify patients:

  • "Have a current and valid license from a recognized New Mexico licensing board which allows diagnosing the qualifying medical condition within their licensure scope of practice…; and,"
  • "Have a New Mexico Controlled Substance number; and,"
  • "Complete required NMDOH training and continuing educational training requirements annually; and,"
  • "Apply, receive, and maintain a valid permit from the NMDOH medical psilocybin program as a diagnostic clinician."

Verbatim from the June 12 recommendation, p. 1.

2Committee inputJune 26 board meeting
AgreementDominic Zurlo (department)

"while this is a Schedule I at this time, because we are treating it in other ways as a medication, it still needs to have that medical clearance… we have had thousands of providers who have referred patients to the program. We are not asking those providers to prescribe psilocybin… We do not see this as any different."

AgreementIan Dunn (board chair)

"This person's job is just to ensure physical safety, that there's no bad drug interactions, that there's no condition that is going to disqualify them or put them at risk."

"we've created a property interest for a group of people who have a financial interest now in providing that care… That is a much more difficult thing to remedy down the line."

The June 25 rules draft does not restate the diagnostic-clinician qualifications; it covers the provider application process, and the CS-number requirement lives only in the June 12 recommendation above. Two clinicians spoke to it at the June 25 committee meeting:

DissentDr. Fatemi (training-program operator)

"in our community, I don't know any any other physicians… who would be also taking the risk… of having a DEA license. And I know we're talking about having a controlled substance license, but that's against federal law."

Connected open threads: the billing line between diagnosis (billable) and treatment (not billable), insurance clawback risk from a risk/benefit attestation, and a suggested rename to "eligibility clinician."

2

The practicum hoursPracticum Requirements

Are 100 hours (facilitators) / 120 hours (practitioners) right, and should every practitioner complete 20 supervisory hours?

What this is: the practicum is the supervised-practice portion of provider training. The number of required hours affects the cost and time to qualify.

Where the two documents differ

The June 12 recommendation (pp. 7-8) sets 100 hours (facilitators) / 120 hours (practitioners) of practicum. The June 25 rules draft (pp. 4-5) does not restate these hours; it reaches practicum only through reciprocity, where a 40-contact-hour exception applies to reciprocal applicants through December 31, 2026.

1The recommendationJune 12, as written
  • "Supervised practice training… must include a minimum of one hundred (100) hours… for Facilitators and one hundred and twenty (120) hours for Practitioners."
  • "Students shall participate in a minimum of eighty (80) hours of administration and same-day therapy sessions… with a minimum of fourteen (14) different patients over a minimum of 8 different… sessions" (≥6 individual with ≥6 patients; ≥2 group with 4+ each).
  • "Students shall participate in a minimum of twenty (20) hours of in-person preparation and post-integration therapy sessions" (≥6 patients; ≥1 group prep and ≥1 group post-integration).
  • "Practitioners must complete an additional minimum of twenty (20) hours as a practitioner supervising facilitators…"
  • "All practicum hours must take place in an approved Healing Center."

Verbatim from the June 12 recommendation, pp. 7-8.

2Committee inputJune 26 board meeting
AgreementDominic Zurlo (department), reading the requirement into the record

"the supervised practice will be 100 hours for facilitators and 120 for practitioners… a minimum of 80 hours of administration and same-day therapy sessions… with a minimum of 14 different patients over eight different… sessions… broken up into six individual and two group sessions."

AgreementDominic Zurlo (department), on where the numbers came from

"the timeframes on this actually have come from various recommendations… including people who have been doing this work underground… this is not just arbitrary."

SuggestionScott Shannon (public; CO psychiatrist and educator)

"40 hours of practicum is probably excessive from what we're seeing… I would encourage a less cautious and conservative approach to this model to try to really emphasize access."

SuggestionLarry Leeman (board member), on cost

"The cheapest one is over $2,000… I think it would be worth trying to model how we think that's going to occur." (Also urged experience in end-of-life and PTSD before sitting with those patients.)

DissentShane (participant), skeptical that rules get revised later

"the change in the future, never happens… I have low confidence that that's going to happen correctly."

3From June 25committee meeting

The June 25 rules draft does not restate the practicum hours. It touches practicum only through reciprocity: a "40 hours of contact time" exception, and a requirement that reciprocal practitioners still "complete the twenty (20) supervisory practicum hours in New Mexico" (rules draft, pp. 4-5). What was said that day:

SuggestionDr. Ann Metz (LPCC; CO clinical facilitator), on the 20 supervisory hours

"Supervision is not a licensing requirement for anyone who's a social worker or counselor. It's an additional credential that people opt into… there is no psilocybin-specific liability insurance product available that covers clinical supervision… creates an uninsurable liability." (Recommended an optional "practitioner supervisor credential.")

AgreementDominic Zurlo (department), declining to remove it

"the facilitators do need to be supervised by the practitioners based upon the model that is created, and that's actually why that is there."

SuggestionCatherine Warnock (licensed practitioner), that instructors should exceed the minimums

"I don't think you should be training 14 experiences. I think 14 experiences is a low number to begin with… maybe a minimum of like 40 people that they have worked with."

SuggestionJames Brown, cautioning against fixed minimums in a new field

"There's not a lot of people pre-set or pre-certified or pre-registered, pre-anything in this. So think about that when you guys talk about minimums."

Where it landed

Set aside on June 26 and sent to July 9. No changes were adopted. Open threads inside this item: whether the 20-hour supervisory practicum is mandatory or opt-in, an instructor / faculty experience floor, the cost modeling Larry Leeman requested, and condition-specific endorsements for end-of-life and PTSD.

3

The reciprocity timelineReciprocity, rules draft pp. 4-5

Are the sunset dates adequate: Dec 31, 2026 for the reduced-hours exception, and Dec 31, 2027 for the department's waiver authority?

What this is: reciprocity lets providers trained in other government-approved programs (for example, Colorado or Oregon) qualify in New Mexico under reduced practicum requirements. The sunset dates set how long those reduced requirements remain available.

Where the two documents differ

Reciprocity and its sunset dates (December 31, 2026 and December 31, 2027) appear only in the June 25 rules draft (pp. 4-5). The June 12 recommendation contains no reciprocity provision.

1The recommendationJune 12

Reciprocity is not in the June 12 recommendation. It exists only in the June 25 rules draft (shown in section 3).

Confirmed against the June 12 recommendation (no reciprocity section).

2Committee inputJune 26 board meeting
AgreementDominic Zurlo (department), on the sunset rationale

"that's actually why that has a sunset clause of the end of December… we are lucky enough to have other states that actually already have programs… those individuals would have to apply by those dates by the end of December."

SuggestionLarry Leeman (board member), on the incentive it creates

"It's almost like we're giving some encouragement for people to get trained in Colorado during reciprocity."

SuggestionDominic Zurlo (department), that the timeline could move

"there's some concerns as far as some of the reciprocity for the timeline that could actually be extended potentially as well, but that we continue those discussions at this point."

3From June 25the rules-draft language

This item's only written language is in the June 25 rules draft:

  • "Exception: any reciprocal applications received by the department by December 31, 2026 will not be required to complete the full practicum hours required by New Mexico, but must demonstrate completion of at least 40 hours of contact time with a minimum of 2 separate individual sessions or 2 separate group sessions…"
  • "Exception: The department reserves the ability to waive, temporarily suspend, or reduce any of the rules regarding the practicum requirements… up until December 31, 2027; in order to facilitate the permitting of individuals trained by other governmental approved programs to build the initial infrastructure of the program."

Verbatim from the June 25 rules draft, reciprocity, pp. 4-5.

ClarificationDr. Ann Metz asked, and Dominic Zurlo answered, at the June 25 committee meeting

Metz: "are people required to complete a training program and have the 40 hour practicum?" Zurlo: "They still need to show the 40 hours… but they would be otherwise exempted from the practica."

Where it landed

Set aside on June 26 and sent to July 9. One clarification landed at June 25: applicants otherwise exempt from the full practicum must still show the 40 contact hours. The dates themselves were set aside, and the department noted the timeline could be extended.
