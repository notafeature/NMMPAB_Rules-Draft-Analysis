How this site is built

Updated July 17, 2026.

What this site is, where every statement comes from, what was produced with AI assistance, and how to report an error.

Read this before relying on anything here

This site is a community reference, not an official source. It is not affiliated with the New Mexico Department of Health or the Medical Psilocybin Advisory Board. Nothing here is final, promulgated rule text, and nothing here is legal or medical advice.

It was built with AI assistance. AI tools handled transcription cleanup, quote extraction, drafting, and cross-checking against the public record, with human review of the result. The meeting transcripts it relies on are unofficial auto-transcripts with speakers inferred from context. Errors of transcription, attribution, or interpretation are possible. Where this site and a primary source disagree, the primary source is right.

Method

Where the content comes from

Every page is built from the public record of the Training and Education rulemaking, in this order of authority:

  • Source documents. The committee's June 12 recommendation, the department's June 25 draft (superseded), and the July 9 rules draft (7.35.3). Draft language on this site is quoted verbatim from these PDFs, and every quote links to its source page. The July 9 draft, the July 9 transcript, and the June 12 recommendation are in the Documents menu above.
  • Meeting transcripts. Full transcripts of the June 25 and June 26 committee meetings and the July 9 Advisory Board meeting. These are unofficial auto-transcripts; speakers were reconstructed from roll-call and hand-off cues. Names garbled by transcription are corrected: Zurlo, Peskuski, Leeman, Dezbaá, Fatemi. Where a speaker or a vote could not be identified, it is left unattributed, including the two votes against the June 26 motion.
  • Live meetings, transcript pending. Statements from the July 16 End-of-Life Care committee meeting and the July 17 Advisory Board meeting, including the revised certifying-clinician language on the CS-number page, are recorded from the live meetings. No transcript or updated draft document has been posted yet; these statements sit in the middle "Transcript" tier below until one is, and will be checked against the posted record when it appears.
  • Out-of-state benchmarks. Comparisons (Oregon, Colorado, Washington D.C., CIIS, the buprenorphine waiver) cite the primary rule or statute for each row, linked at the point of use.

Accuracy

How verified each kind of statement is

Verbatim

Draft-rule and recommendation quotes are copied from the source PDFs and can be checked one click away. Strongest tier.

Transcript

Meeting quotes and outcomes come from the unofficial transcripts. Wording is faithful to the auto-transcript, which itself may contain errors; speaker attribution is inferred.

Compiled

Benchmarks and background are sourced to the cited primary document but were collected and summarized with AI assistance; a citation error is possible.

Known soft points are flagged in place rather than smoothed over. One stands right now. The June 26 motion is attributed to Brenda Burgard per the meeting-note summary, because the raw transcript is unlabeled at that segment.

Policy

What this site will and will not do

  • It records dated events and attributed quotes. It does not characterize how people felt or what they meant.
  • It explains facts and distinctions. It does not recommend outcomes, and no page argues a position.
  • When the record changes, pages are updated and the revision is noted on the page.

Corrections

Report an error

If anything here is wrong, misattributed, or out of date, send it through the community input form with the topic "A correction to this site." Corrections are checked against the primary source and fixed on the page, with the revision noted.
