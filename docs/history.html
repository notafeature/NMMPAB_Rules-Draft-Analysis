  2. July 17, 2026

    Full Advisory Board Hours deferred, 7-0

    Practicum and didactic hours: deferred by a 7-0 vote. The board voted, with no objection, to send the practicum and didactic hours to the Training and Education Committee to develop updated standards.

    Controlled-substance number: kept by the department. Dominic Zurlo said that after taking the earlier concerns to the Secretary of Health and the department’s attorneys, the certifying-clinician controlled-substance-number requirement will remain in the rule the department puts forward, as a medical program consistent with medical cannabis. The board did not vote to remove it; Chris Peskuski restated his objection that it is an access bottleneck and puts liability on a clinician who is not in the room.

    Certifying-clinician exam: revised language. The department read in updated language adding three ways to meet the in-person requirement: an in-person exam within six months, or a telemedicine evaluation paired with either a records review or a formal consult with a clinician who examined the patient in person within six months. It is meant to ease rural and end-of-life access. The verbatim language is on the CS-number page.

    Also on the record: the requirement is the state controlled-substance number, not the federal Schedule I registration; Larry Leeman said federal rescheduling to Schedule III, which he considers likely by mid-2027, could reopen the requirement; and the board asked the department to find how many New Mexico providers hold prescriptive authority without the state number.

    Source: July 17, 2026 Advisory Board meeting; the department posts recordings publicly. Not final rule text; the rule goes to a public hearing.

  3. July 9, 2026

    The tabled items taken up Reciprocity resolved

    Reciprocity was resolved without objection, extending both waiver deadlines to December 31, 2027. The board voted to table the practicum and didactic hours and send them to the Training and Education Committee (July 17). The controlled-substance number for the certifying clinician was sent back to the department to weigh alternatives. Also on the record: "diagnostic clinician" was renamed "certifying clinician" throughout the draft; AED certification was added alongside BLS and CPR in individual training, and EMT certification was added as an outdoor-location staffing alternative; Keenan Ryan appeared as the new Medicaid seat.

    Source: July 9 meeting transcript (unofficial) · July 9 rules draft.

▸ How this process works

The Training and Education Committee writes a recommendation. At an Advisory Board meeting with a quorum, the board votes to accept it and submit it to the Department of Health. The department turns it into rule language, which then goes through the public rule-hearing process before it is promulgated. The department is not obligated to adopt the recommendation as written.

"once we have a thumbs up here from the board… we will be turning this into the rule and then it will still go through and continue to go through the public process of rule hearings and going through all of that."Dominic Zurlo, department, June 26 board meeting

  1. June 26, 2026

    The board vote, 3-2

    The Advisory Board voted 3-2 to accept the recommendation and set three items aside: (1) the controlled-substance number for the certifying clinician, (2) the practicum and didactic hours (practicum 100 for facilitators, 120 for practitioners, plus 20 supervisory), and (3) the reciprocity timeline. Only Larry Leeman's "reluctant yes" is named on the record; the two votes against are not.

    Source: June 26 board meeting transcript.
